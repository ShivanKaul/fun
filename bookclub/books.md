## Flights
- author: Olga Tokarczuk, tr. Jennifer Croft
- accent: #476c78
- cover: https://covers.openlibrary.org/isbn/9780525534198-L.jpg
- tags: literary, fragmentary, travel, philosophical
- sell: I loved it! My first Olga T book. I loved the "liminal" nature of the stories. Very readable.
- quote:
- angle: Is constant movement liberation, avoidance, or a modern spiritual condition?
- why pick: It is the most formally interesting choice: short sections, recurring obsessions, and a structure that invites argument about what makes a novel cohere.
- why not pick: It is not plot-driven; the pleasure is associative.
- link: NYT review | https://www.nytimes.com/2018/08/14/books/review/olga-tokarczuk-flights.html
- link: Guardian | https://www.theguardian.com/books/2017/may/17/flights-olga-tokarczuk-review

## Parable of the Sower
- author: Octavia E. Butler
- accent: #7a5a22
- cover: https://covers.openlibrary.org/isbn/9781538732182-L.jpg
- tags: dystopian, climate fiction, political, classic SF
- sell: I've heard so much about this book, it's been on my list forever. I don't know much about it except there is a dystopian society and that I've loved everything by Octavia Butler.
- quote:
- angle: When institutions fail, what kinds of belief systems become useful?
- why pick: This is the most urgent pick: accessible, gripping, and almost uncomfortably contemporary in its treatment of climate instability, privatization, inequality, and migration.
- why not pick: It is bleak and violent, though not gratuitous.

## Blindsight
- author: Peter Watts
- accent: #47424f
- cover: https://covers.openlibrary.org/isbn/9780765319647-L.jpg
- tags: hard sci-fi, first contact, consciousness, horror
- sell: This book is "hard science fiction" and pretty dense to read but it discusses the relationship between intelligence and consciousness in a way that is uncannily relevant for our modern AI angst.
- quote:
- angle: Is consciousness adaptive, or just a convincing story the brain tells itself?
- why pick: It would produce the most argument. The book is cerebral, creepy, and idea-forward, with a genuinely alien encounter rather than a dressed-up human one.
- why not pick: The most difficult and polarizing pick.
- link: WIRED | https://www.wired.com/2023/10/geeks-guide-peter-watts/
- link: Grimdark Magazine | https://www.grimdarkmagazine.com/review-blindsight-by-peter-watt/
