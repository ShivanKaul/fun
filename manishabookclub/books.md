## The Covenant of Water
- author: Abraham Verghese
- accent: #8a4b36
- cover: https://covers.openlibrary.org/isbn/9780802162175-L.jpg
- tags: Literary, Family saga, India, Historical
- sell: A sweeping, emotionally direct multi-generation novel about family, medicine, faith, inheritance, and grief.
- angle: What do families inherit besides property, names, and stories?
- caveat: It is the longest and most traditional pick.
- why: It gives the group an expansive, character-rich novel with a lot to talk about: caste, Christianity in Kerala, illness, medical ethics, marriage, and the architecture of family secrets.
- link: NYT review | https://www.nytimes.com/2023/05/02/books/review/abraham-verghese-covenant-of-water.html
- link: Washington Post | https://www.washingtonpost.com/books/2023/05/02/covenant-water-abraham-verghese-review/

## Flights
- author: Olga Tokarczuk, tr. Jennifer Croft
- accent: #476c78
- cover: https://covers.openlibrary.org/isbn/9780525534198-L.jpg
- tags: Literary, Fragmentary, Travel, Philosophical
- sell: A sharp, strange constellation of fragments about bodies, motion, maps, death, and modern rootlessness.
- angle: Is constant movement liberation, avoidance, or a modern spiritual condition?
- caveat: It is not plot-driven; the pleasure is associative.
- why: It is the most formally interesting choice: short sections, recurring obsessions, and a structure that invites argument about what makes a novel cohere.
- link: NYT review | https://www.nytimes.com/2018/08/14/books/review/olga-tokarczuk-flights.html
- link: Guardian | https://www.theguardian.com/books/2017/may/17/flights-olga-tokarczuk-review

## Parable of the Sower
- author: Octavia E. Butler
- accent: #7a5a22
- cover: https://covers.openlibrary.org/isbn/9781538732182-L.jpg
- tags: Dystopian, Climate fiction, Political, Classic SF
- sell: A terrifyingly relevant, propulsive novel about collapse, survival, religion, and building meaning amid catastrophe.
- angle: When institutions fail, what kinds of belief systems become useful?
- caveat: It is bleak and violent, though not gratuitous.
- why: This is the most urgent pick: accessible, gripping, and almost uncomfortably contemporary in its treatment of climate instability, privatization, inequality, and migration.
- link: Classic NYT review | https://lithub.com/read-a-classic-review-of-octavia-butlers-parable-of-the-sower/
- link: WIRED | https://www.wired.com/1995/10/when-the-lights-go-out/

## Blindsight
- author: Peter Watts
- accent: #47424f
- cover: https://covers.openlibrary.org/isbn/9780765319647-L.jpg
- tags: Hard sci-fi, First contact, Consciousness, Horror
- sell: A dense, unnerving first-contact novel that turns consciousness, intelligence, and humanity into open problems.
- angle: Is consciousness adaptive, or just a convincing story the brain tells itself?
- caveat: The most difficult and polarizing pick.
- why: It would produce the most argument. The book is cerebral, creepy, and idea-forward, with a genuinely alien encounter rather than a dressed-up human one.
- link: WIRED | https://www.wired.com/2023/10/geeks-guide-peter-watts/
- link: Grimdark Magazine | https://www.grimdarkmagazine.com/review-blindsight-by-peter-watt/
